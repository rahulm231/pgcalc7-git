Every student and every teacher benefits from Annual Giving

Tuition provides only 75% of the cost of a St. Albans education; contributions to Annual Giving and draw from the endowment make up the balance, which is approximately $10,000 per student in 2014-15. In this way, AG directly supports teachers and learners each and every day.

List of 6 items.

  • Academic Enrichment

    Your gift benefits student learning, faculty teaching, and collaborative research by funding academic departments, curriculum development, library acquisitions, technology services, and the academic centers.

  • Financial Aid

    Your gift sustains the School's need-blind admission policy so that a St. Albans education is accessible to all academically-qualified students who are most able to benefit from and contribute to this community–regardless of their ability to pay.

  • Athletics

    Your gift helps foster a culture of intellectual, physical, and personal development by underwriting varsity and club teams, intramural programs, classes, and recreational activities.

  • Campus & Facilities

    Your gift provides funding for the maintenance, grounds- and house-keeping, upgrades, and beautification that the campus and facilities require in order to meet the living and learning needs of our community.

  • Student Life & Spirituality

    Your gift supports opportunities and resources so the St. Albans experience is one that educates the whole student on how to use their spiritual, intellectual, artistic, and physical gifts in service of the grater community.

  • Greatest Need

    By contributing to Annual Giving, you allow the School to determine where do direct your gift so that it will have the greatest impact.

Phone: 202-537-6435

Located in Washington D.C., St. Albans School is a private, all boys day and boarding school. For more than a century, St. Albans has offered a distinctive educational experience for young men in grades 4 through 12. While our students reach exceptional academic goals and exhibit first-rate athletic and artistic achievements, as an Episcopal school we place equal emphasis upon moral and spiritual education.
