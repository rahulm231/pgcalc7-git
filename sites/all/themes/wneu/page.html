Alumni Association

Get involved! You can run for a position on our Board of Directors or volunteer for an activity.

Our Mission

The mission of the Alumni Association is to communicate with and bring value to the alumni body and to support actively and financially the goals of the University as detailed in the University's Mission Statement.

Constitution & Bylaws

Constitution & Bylaws

This Constitution may be amended by a vote of two-thirds of The Association Board of Directors (as defined in Article IV, Section 1 of the Constitution) present at any meeting, provided any proposed amendment has been stated in the notice calling such meeting, and is further subject to the approval of majority vote of the Board of Trustees of the University. Any changes to, or deletions of, existing bylaws, or creation of new bylaws must be approved by a two-thirds vote of The Association Board of Directors and a majority vote of the Board of Trustees of the University.

Skookum Awards

Excellence Personified

The Skookum Award recognizes the commitment and dedicated service of Western New England University students throughout their college career by their demonstrated leadership roles on campus, community service/volunteer experiences, and participation in athletics and cocurricular programs.

  • A Special Appearance at an Alumni Association Meeting

    Baja Vehicle was funded by an alumni grant

    David Greenslade ’14/G'15, cofounder of the University’s Society of Automotive Engineers Baja team, showed off the team’s Mini Baja off-road vehicle to the Alumni Association Board of Directors at a recent meeting. The team, a recipient of an Alumni Association grant, designed and built the vehicle. Pictured in the car is Alumni Association President Mat Nelson ’93. Standing (L-R) are Director of Alumni Relations Kathy Pappas G’98, Member-at-Large Courtney T. Chafer ’14, Greenslade, Members-at-Large Shaine Spencer ’16 and Tim Conrod ’11/G’12, Secretary Scott Davis ’75, Members-at-Large Andrea Bonavita Tarpey ’90, and Laurie Gibson ’92/G’00, and Vice President Ed Lavoie ’99.
